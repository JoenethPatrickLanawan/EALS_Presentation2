  • The Carboniferous Period was a time of dramatic changes in Earth's environment and life.
  • Famous for its vast swampy forests
  • Known for its thick coal deposits that formed over millions of years
  • Named after the Latin word "carbo" which means "coal" or "coal-bearing"

  • Started 359.2 million years ago
  • Ended 299 million years ago
  • Lasted 60 million years

  • Warm and humid by Early Carboniferous
  • Widespread tropical swamps
  • Glaciers formed by Late Carboniferous (causing climate cooling)

  • The Permian Period was the last period within the Paleozoic Era
  • Ended with the Permian-Triassic extinction event
  • Named after the Perm Region in Russia

  • Started 298.9 million years ago
  • Ended 252.17 million years ago
  • Lasted 46.73 million years

  • Sir Roderick Impey Murchison (Scottish Geologist)
  • Coined back in 1841
  • Studied the geology of the Perm Region
  • He found distictive rock layers under the region

    • Sir Roderick Impey Murchison (1856)


  • Cooler and seasonal by Early Permian
  • Became much hotter by the Middle Permian
  • Hot and dry by Late Permian due to the Siberian Traps

  • The Great Dying was the largest mass extinction in Earth's history
  • Also known as the Permian-Triassic extinction event
  • Occurred at the end of the Permian Period
  • Caused by the Siberian Traps
  • 96% of all marine species went extinct
  • 70% of terrestrial vertebrate species went extinct

  • Volcanic eruptions (Siberian Traps)
  • Large-scale wildfires
  • Acid rain
  • Greenhouse effect

  • Started 252.17 million years ago
  • Ended 251.902 million years ago
  • Lasted 170,000 years

  • Coal forests flourish
  • Amphibian Explosion (population)
  • Existence of giant insects and anthropods
  • First Amniotes Evolve
  • Appearance of the first Reptiles
  • Glaciation begins in Gondwana

  • Formation of Pangaea
  • Climate transforms
  • Collapse of Coal forests
  • Synapsid domination
  • Insect evolution
  • End-Permian Extinction
